NYC Bike Routes 2015

NYC's bike lane network has grown extensively over the years. Hover over a lane below to see the date that it was installed. This map was created using the publically accessible bike route shapefile from the NYC DOT Website

This site is based on my hypothesis that Manhattan and Northern Brooklyn are the recipients of the bulk of new bike lane construction in NYC. There is a certain logic for developing bike lanes from a central node, however it does have implications for equity. The outer boroughs such as Queens and the Bronx stack up in terms of lane milage, but these lanes tend to be older and built to previous design standards, which in turn offers less protection than newer protected class lanes. This data also might help community activists such as QNS.BIKE target greater neighborhood connectivity.

This project was made possible using data from the NYC DOT
